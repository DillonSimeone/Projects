Cassardis

Cassardis is the hero's hometown. It's a very normal village, aside from something that happens at the beginning of the game that sets you on your adventure. There is more than a dozen of villagers in the village, each with their own eachday rounttime. It have a citywall, and a watchtower to ensure that the goblins and wolves outside won't attack the village.

Cassardis is a very important part of the game in how it gives you access to a few different locations, such as The Black Isles, the Dripstone Cave, and if you've already beaten the game and are now on your new game plus run, a port-stone connected to the UR-dragon's graveyard.

There is plenty to do and see in Cassardis. Points of interests:

  • 1. Aestella's: A shop.
  • 2. Barn: Gathering point for materials.
  • 3. Benita's: Another shop!
  • 4. Chief's house: The village's leader's house.
  • 5. Fisherman's House: One of the village's family's houses..
  • 6. Heraldo's Grocery: Another shop!
  • 7. Inex's Alehouse: A pub!
  • 8. Iola's house: More shops!
  • 9. Pablos' inn: The cheapest inn in the kingdom
  • 10. Rift stone: A stone for Arisens to summon pawns from.
  • 11. Starfall Bay: The stone that takes you to the UR-Dragon spawns here during New Game Plus.
  • 12. Village Chapel: A small chapel. Your first quest will most likely be from this place.
  • 13. Pier: If you have the DLCs, a woman will appear here and ask if she can take you to the black isles to destroy the darkness within the dark dunegons there.
  • 14. Well to Dripstone cave: A shortcut to witchwoods once you survive the horrors within!
  • 15: Arisen's house: Your avater's house.
